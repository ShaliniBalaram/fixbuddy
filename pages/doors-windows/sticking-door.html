Fix a Sticking Door

Learn how to identify and fix common causes of sticking doors, from humidity issues to misalignment.

Safety Warning

Use proper safety equipment when sanding or planing doors. Wear a dust mask and safety glasses. Be careful with power tools. Get help when removing heavy doors from hinges.

Tools & Materials

  • Screwdriver set
  • Plane or sander
  • Pencil
  • Level
  • Shims
  • Dust mask
  • Safety glasses
  • Work gloves

Diagnosis & Solutions

Identify the Problem

  • Mark sticking points
  • Check seasonal patterns
  • Test door swing
  • Inspect frame alignment

Frame Issues

  • Check frame square
  • Tighten hinges
  • Add shims if needed
  • Adjust strike plate

Door Edge Problems

  • Sand or plane edges
  • Work gradually
  • Test frequently
  • Seal exposed wood

Humidity Solutions

  • Use dehumidifier
  • Improve ventilation
  • Apply moisture barrier
  • Consider weather stripping

Prevention Tips

  • Control room humidity
  • Check alignment seasonally
  • Keep hinges tight
  • Use door stops
  • Monitor weather effects
  • Maintain proper finish
  • Address issues early
  • Regular inspections

Common Issues

Seasonal Changes

Wood doors may expand in humid weather and contract in dry conditions. Consider humidity control solutions.

Foundation Settlement

If multiple doors are affected, foundation issues might be the cause. Consult a structural expert.

Paint Build-up

Multiple paint layers can cause sticking. Sand edges carefully to restore proper clearance.
