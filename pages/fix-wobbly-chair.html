How to Fix a Wobbly Chair

A wobbly chair is not only annoying but can also be dangerous. Follow this guide to stabilize your chair quickly and safely.

Warning: Always work on a stable surface and ensure all joints are completely dry before applying weight. For antique or valuable furniture, consult a professional furniture restorer.

Tools and Materials

  • Wood glue
  • Screwdriver
  • Sandpaper
  • Wood shims (if needed)
  • Clamps

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Identify the Problem

    Check which leg is causing the wobble and inspect all joints.

  2. Tighten All Screws

    Use a screwdriver to tighten any loose screws or bolts.

  3. Check for Uneven Legs

    Place the chair on a flat surface and identify any legs that don't touch the ground.

  4. Fix Loose Joints

    Apply wood glue to loose joints and clamp until dry.

  5. Add Shims if Needed

    Use wood shims to level any remaining unevenness.

Pro Tips

  • Work on a flat surface to accurately assess the wobble
  • Let glue dry completely (24 hours) before using the chair
  • Consider adding felt pads to protect your floor

Safety Notes

  • Wear gloves when handling sharp tools
  • Work in a well-ventilated area when using glue
  • Test the chair's stability before regular use
