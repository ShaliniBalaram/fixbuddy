Install GFCI Outlet

Learn how to safely install or replace a GFCI outlet to protect against electrical shock hazards.

Safety Warning

Working with electrical components can be extremely dangerous. Always turn off power at the circuit breaker and verify with a voltage tester before beginning work. If unsure, consult a licensed electrician.

Tools & Materials

  • GFCI outlet
  • Voltage tester
  • Screwdrivers
  • Wire strippers
  • Needle-nose pliers
  • Electrical tape
  • Wire nuts
  • Safety glasses and gloves

Installation Steps

Step 1: Safety First

  • Turn off power at circuit breaker
  • Test outlet with voltage tester
  • Remove outlet cover plate
  • Double-check power is off

Step 2: Remove Old Outlet

  • Unscrew outlet from box
  • Note wire connections
  • Label wires if needed
  • Remove old outlet

Step 3: Connect GFCI

  • Identify line and load terminals
  • Connect ground wire (green/bare)
  • Connect neutral wire (white)
  • Connect hot wire (black)

Step 4: Complete Installation

  • Secure wires in box
  • Mount GFCI outlet
  • Attach cover plate
  • Test GFCI operation

Common Problems

GFCI Won't Reset

Solution: Check wire connections and ensure power is on

Reversed Wiring

Solution: Double-check line/load connections

Frequent Tripping

Solution: Check for moisture or overloaded circuit

Maintenance Tips

  • Test GFCI monthly
  • Keep outlet clean and dry
  • Check for loose connections
  • Replace if test button fails
  • Label GFCI-protected outlets
