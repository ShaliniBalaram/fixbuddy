How to Fix a Leaky Faucet

Beginner Friendly 30-60 minutes

Learn how to fix common faucet leaks with these simple steps. Most faucet repairs can be completed in under an hour with basic household tools.

Before You Start

Turn off water supply under sink
Place cloth over drain to catch small parts
Take pictures as you disassemble

Common Problems & Solutions

Water Dripping from Spout

What's Wrong?

Water keeps dripping even when the faucet is turned off

How to Fix It:

  1. Turn off water under sink using the knob
  2. Pop off the decorative cap on handle (usually marked H/C)
  3. Unscrew handle with screwdriver
  4. Replace the rubber seal inside (it's a small, flat rubber disc)

No new seal? Use plumber's tape wrapped around the old one as a temporary fix

Water Leaking Around Handle

What's Wrong?

Water seeps out around the base of the handle when using

How to Fix It:

  1. Turn off water supply
  2. Remove handle same way as above
  3. Tighten the packing nut (the large ring under handle)
  4. If still leaking, wrap thread seal tape around it

Can't remove handle? Try spraying with vinegar to loosen mineral buildup

Wobbly or Loose Faucet

What's Wrong?

Entire faucet moves or feels unstable when using

How to Fix It:

  1. Look under sink for large plastic nuts
  2. Hand-tighten these mounting nuts
  3. If needed, give quarter turn with pliers

No pliers? Most mounting nuts can be tightened enough by hand

What You'll Need

Pliers
Or: Clean rag and strong grip
Screwdriver
Or: Butter knife for flat screws
Thread seal tape
Or: Clean cloth for temporary fix

Simple Maintenance Tips

Clean Mineral Buildup

Every 3 months, wrap faucet head in vinegar-soaked cloth for 1 hour to prevent clogs

Every 3 months

Check Handle Tightness

Gently test handles for looseness and tighten if needed - prevents water seepage

Monthly

Inspect Supply Lines

Look under sink for any moisture or drips along the supply lines

Every 6 months
