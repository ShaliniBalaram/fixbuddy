How to Fix a Running Toilet

Beginner Friendly 30-45 minutes

A running toilet can waste hundreds of gallons of water and increase your water bill. Learn how to diagnose and fix this common problem with our step-by-step guide.

Common Problems & Solutions

Faulty Flapper

The most common cause of a running toilet is a worn-out or misaligned flapper that doesn't create a proper seal.

Solution:

  1. Turn off water supply
  2. Flush toilet to empty tank
  3. Inspect flapper for wear or damage
  4. Clean flapper and valve seat
  5. Replace flapper if necessary

Float Issues

If the float is set too high, water will continuously flow into the overflow tube.

Solution:

  1. Remove tank lid
  2. Locate float arm or float cup
  3. Adjust float height
  4. Test flush and adjust as needed

Fill Valve Problems

A malfunctioning fill valve can cause continuous running or improper tank filling.

Solution:

  1. Turn off water supply
  2. Remove old fill valve
  3. Install new fill valve
  4. Adjust water level
  5. Test flush multiple times

What You'll Need

Adjustable wrench
Screwdriver
Replacement flapper
Fill valve (if needed)
Cleaning supplies

Simple Maintenance Tips

Regularly check for leaks by adding food coloring to the tank - if it appears in the bowl without flushing, you have a leak

Clean the rim jets and siphon jet every 6 months to prevent mineral buildup

Replace the flapper every 4-5 years as preventive maintenance

Check the water level in the tank periodically to ensure proper flush performance
