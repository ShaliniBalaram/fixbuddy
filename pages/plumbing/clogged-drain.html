How to Fix a Clogged Drain

Beginner Friendly 15-45 minutes

Clogged drains are a common household problem that can often be resolved without calling a plumber. Follow our guide to clear your drains safely and effectively.

Common Problems & Solutions

Hair and Soap Scum

The most common cause of bathroom drain clogs is accumulated hair and soap residue.

Solution:

  1. Remove drain cover
  2. Use a drain snake or zip-it tool
  3. Pull out visible debris
  4. Flush with hot water
  5. Use enzymatic cleaner monthly

Kitchen Grease Buildup

Kitchen drains often clog due to grease and food particles accumulating over time.

Solution:

  1. Pour boiling water down drain
  2. Add baking soda
  3. Follow with vinegar
  4. Wait 15 minutes
  5. Flush with hot water

Deep Clogs

When simple methods don't work, the clog may be deeper in the pipe.

Solution:

  1. Use a plunger first
  2. Try a drain auger
  3. Remove and clean P-trap
  4. Consider chemical cleaner
  5. Call professional if needed

What You'll Need

Plunger
Drain snake/auger
Drain cleaner
Rubber gloves
Bucket

Simple Maintenance Tips

Use drain strainers to catch hair and debris before they enter the drain

Flush drains weekly with hot water to prevent buildup

Never pour grease or oil down kitchen drains

Use enzymatic cleaners monthly for preventive maintenance
