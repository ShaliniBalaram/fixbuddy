How to Fix a Leaky Sink

Overview

A leaky sink can waste water and potentially cause water damage to your cabinets and home. This guide will help you identify and fix common sink leaks, including faucet drips, drain leaks, and pipe connections.

Tools and Materials Needed

  • Adjustable wrench
  • Pliers
  • Plumber's tape
  • Replacement O-rings or washers
  • Plumbing putty
  • Bucket
  • Towels

Safety Precautions

  • Turn off the water supply before starting any repairs
  • Place a bucket under the work area to catch water
  • Avoid using excessive force when tightening connections
  • Keep the work area dry to prevent slips

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Identify the Leak Source

    Dry the area completely and use paper towels to track where water appears. Common leak points include the faucet base, handles, drain flange, and under-sink connections.

  2. Fix a Faucet Drip

    Turn off water supply, remove handle and cartridge, replace O-rings or washers, reassemble carefully.

  3. Repair Drain Leaks

    Remove old plumber's putty, clean surfaces, apply new putty, tighten drain flange.

  4. Fix Pipe Connections

    Tighten connections, replace plumber's tape, ensure proper alignment.

  5. Test the Repair

    Turn water back on slowly, check for leaks, monitor for 24 hours.

Troubleshooting

  • If leak persists after tightening, check for damaged components
  • Multiple leak points may indicate need for fixture replacement
  • Mineral buildup can prevent proper sealing
  • Cracks in porcelain require professional repair

Maintenance Tips

  • Regularly check under-sink areas for moisture
  • Clean aerators every 6 months
  • Avoid hanging heavy items on faucets
  • Address small leaks promptly to prevent bigger issues

Note: If you're unsure about any step or encounter difficulties, don't hesitate to contact a professional plumber. Water damage can be expensive to repair, so it's better to be safe than sorry.
