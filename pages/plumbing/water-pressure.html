Fix Water Pressure Issues

Learn how to diagnose and fix common water pressure problems in your home.

Safety Warning

Never adjust main water pressure beyond recommended levels (40-80 PSI). Turn off water supply before working on pipes or fixtures. For major pressure issues or municipal water problems, contact your water company or a licensed plumber.

Tools & Materials

  • Water pressure gauge
  • Adjustable wrench
  • Pipe wrench
  • Screwdriver set
  • Plumber's tape
  • Cleaning supplies
  • Safety gloves
  • Bucket

Diagnosis & Solutions

Low Pressure in One Fixture

  • Check aerator for clogs
  • Clean fixture head
  • Inspect shut-off valve
  • Check supply line

Low Pressure Throughout Home

  • Test main pressure
  • Check pressure reducer
  • Inspect main shut-off
  • Look for leaks

Sudden Pressure Changes

  • Check for pipe damage
  • Inspect water meter
  • Test pressure tank
  • Monitor usage patterns

High Pressure Problems

  • Install pressure reducer
  • Adjust existing regulator
  • Check expansion tank
  • Monitor pipe condition

Prevention Tips

  • Monitor pressure regularly
  • Clean fixtures monthly
  • Check for leaks
  • Maintain water softener
  • Service pressure regulator
  • Keep pipes insulated
  • Document pressure changes
  • Schedule annual checks

Common Issues

Time-Based Problems

Low pressure during peak usage hours might indicate municipal supply issues or undersized pipes.

Seasonal Changes

Pressure variations with temperature changes could suggest pipe expansion or freezing issues.

Water Softener Impact

Pressure drops during regeneration cycles are normal but should be temporary.
