Sharpen Dull Kitchen Knives

Learn how to safely and effectively sharpen your kitchen knives to restore their cutting edge.

Safety Warning

Always exercise extreme caution when handling sharp knives. Work slowly and deliberately to avoid injury.

Tools & Materials

  • Whetstone or sharpening stone
  • Honing steel
  • Clean cloths
  • Water
  • Knife guide (optional)
  • Cutting board
  • Safety gloves
  • Paper for testing

Sharpening Steps

Step 1: Prepare Tools

  • Soak whetstone if required
  • Set up stable work surface
  • Clean knife thoroughly
  • Put on safety gloves

Step 2: Find Angle

  • Identify bevel angle
  • Position knife correctly
  • Use guide if needed
  • Maintain consistent angle

Step 3: Sharpen Edge

  • Start with coarse grit
  • Draw blade across stone
  • Maintain steady pressure
  • Count strokes per side

Step 4: Finish and Test

  • Switch to fine grit
  • Hone with steel
  • Test sharpness
  • Clean and dry knife

Common Problems

Uneven Edge

Solution: Maintain consistent angle and pressure

Not Sharp Enough

Solution: Progress through grits properly

Over-sharpening

Solution: Count strokes and test frequently

Maintenance Tips

  • Use cutting boards only
  • Hone regularly
  • Store knives properly
  • Clean after each use
  • Avoid dishwasher
