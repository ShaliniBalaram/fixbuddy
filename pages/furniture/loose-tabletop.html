Fix a Loose Tabletop

Learn how to securely reattach and stabilize loose tabletops to their bases.

Safety Warning

Tables can be heavy and awkward to handle. Get help when flipping or moving large tables. Use proper safety equipment when working with tools and adhesives. Keep the work area clear to prevent accidents.

Tools & Materials

  • Screwdriver set
  • Wood glue
  • Corner brackets
  • Mounting screws
  • Wood clamps
  • Drill with bits
  • Measuring tape
  • Safety gloves

Fix Steps

Step 1: Assess the Problem

  • Examine current attachment method
  • Check for damaged hardware
  • Look for loose or stripped screws
  • Inspect for wood damage

Step 2: Prepare for Repair

  • Clear and clean work area
  • Carefully flip table over
  • Remove old hardware
  • Clean attachment surfaces

Step 3: Reattach Tabletop

  • Apply wood glue if needed
  • Install new brackets
  • Add reinforcement screws
  • Align properly

Step 4: Secure and Test

  • Tighten all hardware
  • Apply clamping pressure
  • Allow glue to dry
  • Test stability

Maintenance Tips

  • Check attachment points monthly
  • Tighten hardware regularly
  • Avoid dragging table
  • Use proper table pads
  • Keep away from moisture
  • Don't overload table
  • Address issues promptly
  • Monitor for movement
