Patch Small Holes in Drywall

Learn how to repair small holes and dents in your drywall for a seamless finish.

Tools & Materials

  • Drywall joint compound
  • Putty knife
  • Sandpaper (medium and fine grit)
  • Drywall tape (for larger holes)
  • Paint and primer
  • Paint brush or roller
  • Drop cloth
  • Safety goggles and dust mask

Fix Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Area

  • Clean around the hole
  • Remove loose material
  • Sand edges smooth
  • Wipe away dust

Step 2: Apply First Coat

  • Fill hole with joint compound
  • Spread evenly with putty knife
  • Feather edges
  • Let dry completely

Step 3: Sand and Second Coat

  • Sand first coat smooth
  • Apply second thin coat
  • Extend beyond first coat
  • Allow to dry

Step 4: Final Touch

  • Sand until smooth
  • Prime patched area
  • Paint to match wall
  • Blend with existing paint

Common Problems

Visible Patch

Solution: Feather edges more gradually and use matching paint sheen

Compound Shrinkage

Solution: Apply multiple thin coats rather than one thick coat

Paint Color Mismatch

Solution: Paint entire wall section from corner to corner

Maintenance Tips

  • Keep leftover paint for touch-ups
  • Use wall anchors for hanging items
  • Address holes promptly to prevent enlargement
  • Store basic repair materials for quick fixes
  • Document paint colors and brands used
