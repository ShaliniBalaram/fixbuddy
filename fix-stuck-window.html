Fix a Stuck Window

Get your window sliding smoothly again

If your window is painted shut, be careful when breaking the paint seal. Wear safety glasses and work gloves to protect yourself from paint chips and broken glass.

  • Putty knife or paint scraper
  • Silicone spray lubricant
  • Sandpaper (medium and fine grit)
  • Clean rags
  • Work gloves
  • Safety glasses

Estimated Time: 30-45 minutes

üìù Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Examine the window:
    • Check if it's painted shut
    • Look for visible damage to the frame or tracks
    • Test if the window lock is engaged
  2. Clean the window tracks:
    • Remove debris with a vacuum
    • Wipe down tracks with a damp cloth
    • Let tracks dry completely
  3. If painted shut:
    • Run putty knife along the seam where window meets frame
    • Gently tap putty knife with hammer if needed
    • Work around entire window frame
  4. Sand rough spots:
    • Use medium grit first for rough areas
    • Follow with fine grit for smooth finish
    • Wipe away dust with clean cloth
  5. Lubricate tracks:
    • Apply silicone spray to tracks
    • Move window up and down to distribute lubricant
    • Wipe away excess
